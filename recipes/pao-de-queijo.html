Pão de Queijo

Pão de Queijo is a traditional food from Minas Gerais, Brazil. It's one of the most delicious food in the world and very famous in Brazil.



Ingredients:

  • 1 cup tapioca flour
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 large egg

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together tapioca flour, Parmesan cheese, and salt.
  3. In a small saucepan, heat oil and milk over medium heat until just simmering. Do not boil.
  4. Pour hot milk mixture into the dry ingredients and stir quickly until a thick dough forms.
  5. Let dough cool slightly for about 5 minutes. Then, stir in the egg until fully incorporated.
  6. Drop tablespoons of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, leaving about 1 inch of space between them.
  7. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown and puffed.
  8. Let cool slightly before serving. Enjoy warm!

Tips:

  • For a chewier Pão de Queijo, use less milk. For a lighter and airier Pão de Queijo, use more milk.
  • You can add other ingredients to your Pão de Queijo dough, such as chopped ham, shredded chicken, or finely chopped vegetables.
  • Pão de Queijo is best enjoyed fresh out of the oven, but they can also be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days.
  • Use the right type of tapioca flour. Not all tapioca flours are created equal.
    For Pão de Queijo, you need to use tapioca starch, also known as tapioca flour or fécula de tapioca. Avoid using tapioca pearls, which are not the same thing.
  • Don't overmix the dough. Overmixing the dough will make your Pão de Queijo tough. Mix the dough just until the ingredients are combined.
  • Let the dough rest. After you add the egg to the dough, let it rest for a few minutes before baking.
    This will allow the tapioca flour to absorb the moisture and make the Pão de Queijo puff up nicely.
  • Don't overcrowd the baking sheet. Give the Pão de Queijo plenty of space to rise in the oven.

With these tips, you're sure to make delicious and authentic Pão de Queijo that everyone will love!
